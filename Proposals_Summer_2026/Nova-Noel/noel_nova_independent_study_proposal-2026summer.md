# Title

Edo Echoes: Interactive Cultural Adaptation and Fictive Worldbuilding through Twine

# Project Overview

This independent study is conducted under the aegis of the Center for Holistic Integration (CHI) at New York City College of Technology, CUNY. CHI supports interdisciplinary meta-projects that integrate research, creative practice, technology, and collaborative experimentation across disciplines. Within this framework, Noel’s project contributes to CHI’s broader investigations into narrative systems, cultural transformation, interactive media, and blended storytelling environments.

This independent study will support the development of an original interactive fiction project by Noel Nova that explores Japanese Edo-period culture through historical research, narrative adaptation, and interactive game design. The project will culminate in a Twine-based non-linear game that transforms selected historical and cultural materials into a fictive narrative integrated within the broader CHI research and project ecosystem.



Rather than reproducing historical events directly, the study will investigate methods for respectfully adapting historical narratives, cultural practices, and symbolic systems into a parallel fictive environment. The project therefore combines cultural research, narrative design, game development, and worldbuilding into a unified interdisciplinary creative work.

---

# Project Concept

The central concept of this independent study is the creation of a playable narrative experience inspired by Japanese Edo-period history and UNESCO-recognized cultural traditions. During the planning discussion, several possible cultural influences were suggested, including:

- Kabuki theatre
- Bunraku puppet theatre
- Fururi Odori ritual dances
- Washi paper craftsmanship
- Edo-period political and social narratives
- Historical transitions surrounding the Tokugawa Shogunate



Particular attention was given to Bunraku puppet theatre because of its conceptual relationship to the Blended Shadow Puppet initiative and broader CHI investigations into puppetry, storytelling, mediated performance, and symbolic environments.

The resulting game will not function as a historical simulation, but rather as a cultural transformation exercise in which core themes, tensions, aesthetics, and symbolic structures are translated into a new fictive world while preserving recognizable cultural inspiration and respect for source material.

# Academic and Creative Objectives

The independent study has several interconnected objectives:

1. Conduct focused research into selected Edo-period cultural practices, historical events, or artistic traditions.

2. Investigate UNESCO intangible cultural heritage materials and their relationship to narrative, ritual, and performance systems.

3. Develop methods for adapting historical and cultural material into fictive narrative environments.

4. Design and implement a non-linear interactive fiction experience using Twine.

5. Explore player agency and knowledge-driven gameplay through branching narrative structures.

6. Contribute a new narrative artifact to the growing Blended Shadow Puppet fictive universe, which serves as a beneficiary and integration framework for CHI's UNESCO-related cultural research projects.

7. Develop professional documentation and project management practices using GitHub-based workflows.

# Research Methodology

The project will proceed through several overlapping stages:

## 1. Cultural and Historical Research

Noel will identify a specific narrative framework, cultural practice, historical moment, or symbolic structure from the Edo period as the basis for adaptation. Research may include:

- UNESCO cultural heritage documentation
- Historical analysis
- Artistic and theatrical traditions
- Ritual practices
- Social and political structures
- Traditional craft systems
- Music and performance traditions

Research notes and references will be documented throughout development.  All research notes will be stored in GitHUb repository, and all research references stored in Zotero.

## 2. Narrative Transformation

After identifying a suitable source narrative or cultural structure, Noel will adapt the material into a parallel fictive world framework. As discussed during the planning meeting, this process involves preserving the structural essence of the original while shifting it into a transformed symbolic context.

The adaptation process may involve:

- Renaming locations and characters
- Reframing political structures
- Translating symbolic systems
- Modifying cosmology or mythology
- Embedding cultural references into fictional environments

The goal is not imitation, but respectful transformation.

## 3. Interactive Narrative Development

The final work will be implemented using Twine as the primary delivery platform. The game will emphasize:

- Branching narrative pathways
- Meaningful player choice
- Knowledge acquisition and discovery
- Exploration-based interaction
- Narrative consequence

The project will specifically avoid purely linear storytelling structures. During the meeting discussion, Noel and Dr. Smith emphasized that player decisions should produce meaningful shifts in understanding and narrative outcome.
